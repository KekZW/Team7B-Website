Our Design Process & Product Overview

Understanding the Problem Statement

We began by identifying key environmental and safety concerns along River Kelvin. Our focus was on public awareness, data clarity, and accessibility of real-time river conditions.

First Partner Meeting

We consulted with stakeholders to validate our goals and ensure alignment with community needs. Feedback from this meeting shaped the core objectives of our design.

Brainstorming

Our team collaboratively explored multiple concepts, discussing potential solutions like alert systems, visual dashboards, and mobile-friendly interfaces.

Storyboard

We sketched out user journeys to visualize interactions with our product â€” from checking river safety before kayaking, to accessing historical water data.

Lo-Fi Design

Initial low-fidelity wireframes were created using Figma. These wireframes allowed quick iteration based on user feedback before investing in visual polish.

First High Fidelity Design

We translated our wireframes into high-fidelity mockups, focusing on visual consistency, intuitive navigation, and responsive layout for all devices.

Final High Fidelity Design

Based on feedback, the final design included enhanced accessibility, clearer icons, and real-time river data integration.

Product + Features

Our final product is a web app called River Kelvin Watch. It enables users to:

  • Check real-time river flow and water level
  • Receive alerts on hazardous conditions
  • Understand data through simplified charts and icons
  • View historical patterns for awareness and planning
