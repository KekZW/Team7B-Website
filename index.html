The Background

Friends of the River Kelvin (FORK) started in 1991 when a group of friends got together to enjoy and explore the River Kelvin. In 1999 the group founded a charity to care for the River Kelvin and it‚Äôs surroundings. Over the years FORK has run monthly clean-ups, citizen science projects and also many music and arts events, community festivals, planted trees, monitored the river and run campaigns. The charity became a Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation in 2020 and FORK intend to build on the legacy of those Friends before them.

Problem Statement

When conducting activities by the river, the condition of the river greatly impacts safety aspects. The Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA) does publish data around river flow, but it is difficult to decipher‚Äîespecially when it comes to predicting river conditions ahead of time. Another aspect is that the river flow rate upstream affects pollution levels downstream, but again, this is difficult to derive from the available data.

Key Points

River condition data can be difficult to understand, especially for non-technical users.

There is a lack of accessible tools to help predict future river conditions based on current and upstream data.
